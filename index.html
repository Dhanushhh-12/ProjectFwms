Our Mission

Building a sustainable future where no food goes to waste.

A Food Waste Management System (FWMS) is a systematic, often technology-driven approach to reducing, redistributing, and responsibly disposing of surplus food. Globally, approximately one-third of all food produced—about 1.3 billion tons—is wasted annually, leading to severe economic losses and environmental damage, such as high methane emissions from landfills. Core Objectives: Modern systems focus on a "Food Recovery Hierarchy" to maximize utility and minimize harm: Source Reduction: Preventing waste at the start through accurate demand forecasting and inventory tracking. Redistribution: Connecting donors (like restaurants or households) with recipients (NGOs, orphanages, or individuals in need) via web or mobile applications. Valorisation: Converting unavoidable waste into useful byproducts like animal feed, compost, or renewable energy (biogas) through anaerobic digestion.
